TreeHacks 2024 Travel Guide

Please read all of this document in its entirety. We cannot make exceptions or grant extensions.

TreeHacks provides buses and flight reimbursements for all accepted hackers. We believe that hacking should be accessible to all students regardless of their background, and travel reimbursements help make that more possible. We are updating our reimbursement procedure to reduce the issues experienced last year and to ensure a more timely and efficient reimbursement process.

There are TWO CASES:

  1. you attend a bus school: if your school is on this list, you will be provided with a coach bus to travel to TreeHacks
  2. you do not attend a bus school: you are eligible for a flight reimbursement up to a regional cap

We are NOT reimbursing other travel expenses (such as Uber/Lyfts, AirBnBs, food, non-TreeHacks provided buses, parking, gas, etc…). We will provide a Slack channel to communicate about ride-shares to/from the airport.

🚌 case 1: bus 🚌

If you attend a bus school, you will have access to a coach bus to travel to the event. You are welcome to travel in other ways to TreeHacks, but you will not be eligible for a travel reimbursement. The coach buses will pick students up from UC Berkeley and UCLA.

Flight reimbursement criteria:

  1. flights must be purchased on Egencia by Jan. 16
  2. you must complete all registration steps listed in your TreeHacks portal by Jan. 16
  3. you must register in-person at the event
  4. you must submit a qualified project to the Devpost before hacking closes

A qualified project is one where:

  • it is clear that thought and effort went into the project
  • it contains original code and is not a fork/clone of an existing repo
  • it adheres to the rules of the hackathon, which require all coding to be done during the 36 hours

If any one of these criteria is not met by the deadline, we will not be able to reimburse your travel.
