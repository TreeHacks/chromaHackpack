TreeHacks 2024 Ultimate Guide

We're pumped to see you at TreeHacks 2024! Here's a comprehensive guide

of everything you need to know about TreeHacks — if we missed anything, shoot us an email at hello@treehacks.com, and we'll help you out asap.

This doc will be updated with more information as we get closer to the event.

📍 time & location 📍

TreeHacks 10 will take place February 16 - 18, 2024 in the Jen-Hsun Huang Engineering Center (“Huang”) at 475 Via Ortega, Stanford, CA 94305. Location here.

Registration will take place outside of Huang. Almost all of our events are in Huang, but we'll have a few that take place elsewhere. Volunteers or organizers will lead you to any other event locations at the time of the event.

📦 packing list 📦 

  • Government-issued ID and university ID
  • You will need to show both forms of ID to be let into the venue
  • Submitted liability waiver
  • Submitted GrantEd registration form
  • Submitted photo and communications release form (will be available for signing at the venue)
  • Computer and accessories (mouse, keyboard, etc.)
  • Chargers (phone, laptop, etc.)
  • Any smartphones or tablets you want to incorporate in your hack
  • Hardware from home that you might want to hack on. We'll have lots of hardware available too.
  • Sleep-related things (sleeping bags, pillow, blankets, etc.)
  • There will be a resting area within the venue itself, but we can’t provide sleeping materials to hackers.
  • A change of clothes & clothes you can move in (for fun activities!)
  • A sweater / jacket in case it gets cold
  • Toiletries
  • Toothbrush/toothpaste
  • Shower supplies (yes, we have showers): soap, shampoo/conditioner, towel, shower shoes
  • Headphones / earbuds 
  • Medication – if needed 
  • Refillable water bottle

🔌 hardware 🔌 

We have a huge supply of hardware for you to hack on! You can see everything and rent it at our hardware checkout site during the weekend of TreeHacks (a complete list coming soon).

We're also excited to have access to the Product Realization Lab, a makerspace at Stanford! The PRL is located in Room 36 in the basement of the Huang Engineering Center. If you're planning to work on a hardware hack, the PRL is an awesome place to build; it has soldering irons, 3D printers, laser cutters, oscilloscopes, materials, tools, and pretty much everything else you'll need to build an incredible hack.

❗ rules ❗

In order to be eligible to win a prize, your team and project must follow all of the guidelines of TreeHacks:

  1. all members of your team must have checked-in to TreeHacks
  2. the members of your team must be physically present to demo your project to judges from 10am - 1pm on Sunday
  3. all building must have taken place during hacking hours (9pm on Friday, Jan. 16 - 9am on Sunday, Jan. 18). We check all Github repos for additions made before or after the hacking period and for any large copy/paste contributions.
  1. Note: ideation and planning is allowed (and encouraged) before the hackathon, but all coding/building must take place during hacking hours

🏆 judging & criteria 🏆

In order to be judged, you'll need to submit your project on Devpost (link coming soon). On the Devpost, you give all the information about your project, including your team and the prizes you'd like to be considered for, including any challenges you might have hacked in.

In order to win a prize, you must be physically present during judging to demo your project to the judges.

First round judging takes place during an expo, where you will present your project to judges as they walk around to you. The expo is also when sponsors decide all the prizes. You will have 2-3 minutes to speak with each judge, so be ready to work through your demo quickly. Also, be sure you're showing your product rather than just a slideshow; hackathons are all about what you learn and build! Along with this, you 100% should still demo even if your project isn't finished.

Finally, the top teams will be announced and present at our closing ceremonies, where they will demo their projects for everyone.

Judging is based on three criteria:

  1. Creativity

We want hackers to create a project that makes you say “wow” and tell all your friends about it. We're looking for projects that think so far outside the box that you begin to wonder why there was a box at all in the first place.

  1. Technical Complexity

In only 36 hours, hackers manage to build projects with remarkably complex infrastructures built on excitingly advanced frameworks. We hope to see projects that are really running some beautiful code or hardware under the hood.

  1. Social Impact

We're looking for hacks that are the blueprints for change that will impact future generations in humanity's most pressing areas of concern in the coming years.
